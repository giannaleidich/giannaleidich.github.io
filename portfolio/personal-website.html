Creating This Website

Introduction

This website is the final project for the software course of the professional writing and technical communications certificate program. It was created using HTML/CSS and some JavaScript. You can read about my design process, including the research, planning, implementation, and usability testing that went into creating this website.

Research

The first step in my design process was to research and gather information from other designerâ€™s portfolio websites. During this stage, I focused on visual design and noted what design features that I liked best and wanted to replicate on my own website.

Planning

During the planning stage, I produced this planning document, in which I define my audience and goals, as well as detail my visual design choices and aesthetics, based on my prior research.

Also included in this doc plan were wireframes and a sitemap, to help guide my implementation and logical flow of the website.

Implementation

Creating the Navbar

The first component that I implemented was the navbar, which is consistent across the entire website. I focused on layout, readability, and responsiveness on a page resize. If the browser window gets too small, the links on the navbar stack due to the limited width real estate.

Designing the Home page

After finalizing the navbar, I focused on slowly building the website page-by-page, naturally starting with the Home page.

This page was the most difficult to implement, because its structure is the most unique. The technique that I relied on the most here was using grid because it allowed me to have specific control over where and how my elements appeared.

Finishing the Remaining pages

The remaining pages were much more straightforward to implement. Each one has a similar structure, especially the About Me and Contact pages.

The only pages to give me real struggle were the Resume and Portfolio pages, due to their complicated layouts. Again I used grid, and sometimes grids inside of grids.

I also decided to use JavaScript to add an image carousel on the Portfolio page to showcase my graphics and add a bit of interactivity to the page.

Conclusion

This website is the result of research, planning, implementing, and testing, done in several iterations. To design and create a website that visually represents me as a designer and that is demonstrative of the various skills that I bring to the table was my main goal in creating this website, and I believe that I have achieved those goals.
